Cookie Policy

What are cookies?

Small text files known as cookies are transferred to or read by your web browser or the memory of your device. A cookie often includes the domain name (Internet address) from which it came, its "lifetime" (the period of time after which it expires), and a randomly generated unique number or other identification. A cookie might also store data about your device, including user preferences, browsing histories, and actions taken while utilizing our services.

Are there different types of cookies?

First-party and third-party cookies
There are cookies from both first- and third-parties. We appoint first-party cookies straight to your device. For instance, we use first-party cookies to customize our App to the preferred language of your browser and to learn more about how you use our App. Our partners and service providers may put third-party cookies on your computer or device. By using the consent management tools on our App and in-app, you can find out more about these partners and service providers. See "How can you control cookies?" below for more information on these tools.
Session and persistent cookies
Cookies come in two varieties: temporary and permanent. Session cookies are only active while your browser is open. Session cookies are used for a variety of purposes, including to gather information about how you use our App during a single browser session and to make our App easier for you to use. Longer-lasting and remaining after the current session are persistent cookies. You can use these types of cookies to rapidly log in to our App once again, for analytics, and for other reasons that are detailed below.

What about other tracking technologies, such as web beacons and SDKs?

Cookies are used for similar objectives as other technologies like web beacons (also known as pixels, tags, or clear gifs), tracking URLs, or software development kits (SDKs). With the use of web beacons, which are tiny graphical files with a unique identifier, we can tell when someone has visited our App or clicked on an email we have sent them. Custom-made connections called tracking URLs allow us to track the sources of visitors to our Apps. Small pieces of code called SDKs are integrated into programs and perform similar tasks to cookies and web beacons. 
In this Cookie Policy, we also refer to these technologies as "cookies" for simplicity's sake.

How can you control cookies?

There are several cookie management options available. Please note that changing your cookie settings may result in a less than satisfactory service as the service may not be personalized. In some cases, you may not even be able to use all or part of our Service.
Tools we provide
You can set and adjust your consent choices at any time, by heading to your account settings in the app and selecting Personalize My Consent Choices.
Browser and device controls
Some web browsers have settings that allow you to control or refuse cookies, or to notify you when cookies are placed on your computer. Each Internet browser has slightly different instructions for managing cookies. Specific instructions can be found in each browser's help menu.
You can also reset your device identifier or opt out of the collection or processing of your identifier by using the appropriate settings on your mobile device. The steps to manage identifiers are slightly different for each device. You can find specific instructions in each device's help or settings menu.
Interest-based advertising tools
Advertising companies may participate in self-regulatory programmes that allow you to opt out of any interest-based ads involving them. For more information on this, you can visit the following sites: Digital Advertising Alliance; Interactive Digital Advertising Alliance; Appchoices (apps only).
Opting out does not mean you will no longer see ads. This means that you will no longer see personalized ads from companies that participate in opt-out programs. If you delete the cookies on your device after opting out, you will still need to opt out again.
